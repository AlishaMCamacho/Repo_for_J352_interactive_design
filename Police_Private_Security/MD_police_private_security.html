Maryland's Private Security Industry Grows in the Face of a Dwindling Police Force

By: Alisha Camacho


Security Guards Outnumber Police Officers in Maryland
Infogram

In response to public reaction and protests over increasing concerns over police misconduct, Maryland state legislators passed the Police Reform and Accountability Act of 2021, which repealed legislation, passed in 1974 giving police special protections. Maryland was the first state to enact and revoke the Law Enforcement Bill of Rights, and at least 29 other states have followed with similar repeals.

Three years later, police departments across the country are still struggling to hire officers. Between 2019 and 2022, police numbers in Maryland dropped by nearly 11%, according to data from the Federal Bureau of Investigation. And as police numbers decline, the private security industry grows. Between 2019 and 2023, the number of security officer employed in Maryland grew by nearly 8%, according to the U.S. Bureau of Labor.

Private security guards today are nearly ubiquitous in American life. They patrol shopping malls and residential neighborhoods. They are night guards in apartment buildings and loss prevention officers at chain stores. Hired by businesses, neighborhoods, and government entities, these guards are charged with protecting their clients, deterring crime, and providing customer service.
