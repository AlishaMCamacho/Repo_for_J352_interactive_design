It's me, Dexter!


I'm not scary. You're scary!


I first met Dashie at Anacostia Park in Washington, DC. I had just traveled from New Hampshire with my new human. We had spent the previous night in a motel in a place called New Jersey. All the smells were different. My skin was feeling itchy in this new place. I didn't know what was happening, or whether I was going back to New Hampshire. I was just hoping my new human wouldn't leave my side. It was all very overwhelming.

After trying to scare Dashie off, I realized she just wanted to play. So we played in the field. I kept an eye over my shoulder the entire time though. You can never be too careful in these situations.

Later that night, Dashie tried to steal my water. She was up to no good and wanted to leave me with nothing. I would die of dehydration, hunger, and loneliness. I couldn't let this happen. I'd been through too much. I had to act.




Photo Caption: My human took Dashie and I and so many hikes together in Fort Dupont near my new home. I loved it and forgot that Dashie was a potential threat. We just had lots of fun.


I was starting to feel less and less like myself. I was angry and scared at everyone except my new human. I still liked meeting new people, but I just didn't feel safe.

Eventually, a nice food lady came to the house, and we spent a lot of time together. I love learning new things and making everyone happy. I also started getting these weird tasting circle things in all my meals.

I cannot believe my first "gotcha day" is around the corner. I love my new home so much. I play with Dashie all the time. And I have a crush on her sister Scootie. She is so nice and pretty. I also get to meet seniors and children at the park and on our walks. Everyone who isn't afraid of me loves me and I love them! Although other dogs still make me weary. But I am beginning to think they are okay after all. We shall see.

Origin Story


My parents are all star dogs from a breeder in New Hampshire. One day, when I was a puppy, this lady Sue picked me up and brought me to a new home. I immediately fell in love with with my new humans, especially the grumpy old man who really wasn't too grumpy. I was a surprise gift to cheer him up after his previous dog passed away.

That might sound like some big shoes to fill, but I was mostly focused on chewing toilet paper, chasing the cats in the house, and digging holes in the yard. It was around this time that I met my (now) new human. She would play with me all the time. It was amazing. The old man couldn't chase me like she could.

Eventually the old man passed away. I was very sad and withdrawn. Sue was worried about me and said I needed a change of scenary. She wasn't wrong.

I didn't realize what was happening at the time, but when my new human picked me up, I sprinted to her car. I was ready to go. I didn't realize where to, or the adventure I was in for, but I'm so grateful to have a new home.
